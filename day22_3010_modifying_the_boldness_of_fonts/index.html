CSS is one of the core languages of the Open Web and is standardised across browsers by the W3C specification. CSS saves a lot of work. Styles can be saved externally by definition. This means that developers do not have to go through the hassle of editing the layout on all pages. CSS is divided into different levels: CSS1 is now deprecated, CSS2.1 is the recommended standard and CSS3 is divided into smaller modules and is in the process of being standardised.
